Tenacity is an easy-to-use, cross-platform
multi-track audio editor/recorder
for Windows, MacOS, GNU/Linux and other operating systems
and is developed by a group of volunteers as open-source software.

Its features include:

  • Recording from audio devices (real or virtual)
  • Export & Import a wide range of audio formats
    (extendible with FFmpeg)
  • High Quality including up to 32-bit float audio support
  • Plug-Ins providing support for VST, LV2, and AU plugins
  • Scripting in the built-in scripting language Nyquist,
    or in Python, Perl and other languages with named pipes
  • Editing arbitrary sampling and multi-track timeline
  • Accessibility including editing via keyboard,
    screen reader support and narration support
  • Tools useful in the analysis of signals, including audio

Getting Help

A great place to ask for general questions with using Tenacity,
starting discussions about your ideas and feature requests,
and generally getting to know the community
is the tenacity-discuss mailing list .

You may also use our IRC channel,
#tenacity (hosted on irc.libera.chat),
where you're welcome to ask questions and have discussions
in a more free-form, real-time context.
The channel is also bridged to Matrix.
